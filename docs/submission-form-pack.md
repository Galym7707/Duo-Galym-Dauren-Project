# Saryna MRV Submission Form Pack

Where we are: as of March 27, 2026, the MVP, demo path, deck outline, and Q&A pack are already in the repo.  
Nearest goal: make the submission on April 3, 2026 easy to complete without rewriting the story every time.  
Main risk: using different wording across the form, pitch, and demo, which weakens credibility.

## One-Line Description

`Saryna is an MRV workflow layer for methane and flaring visibility that turns satellite screening into incidents, verification tasks, and exportable evidence for Kazakhstan oil and gas teams.`

## 250-Character Version

`Saryna helps Kazakhstan oil and gas teams turn methane and flaring screening signals into incidents, verification tasks, and MRV-ready reports. It is a workflow product, not just a dashboard.`

## 500-Character Version

`Saryna is a web-based MRV workflow tool for methane and flaring visibility in Kazakhstan oil and gas. We use satellite screening as the starting point, then convert anomalies into owned incidents, verification tasks, and exportable MRV evidence. Our product is positioned as a screening and operational prioritization layer, not a replacement for LDAR.`

## Problem Statement

Short:

`Oil and gas teams often have visibility tools, but not one workflow that turns an abnormal methane or flare signal into accountable action and defensible reporting.`

Medium:

`Methane and flaring signals are increasingly important for operations, ESG, and compliance, but the workflow is often fragmented. Teams may have maps, spreadsheets, and separate reporting processes, but not one system that connects screening, ownership, verification, and MRV evidence. This slows response and weakens auditability.`

## Solution

Short:

`Saryna connects methane and flare screening with incident management, verification tasks, and MRV-ready reporting.`

Medium:

`Saryna is an MRV workflow layer that helps teams move from anomaly detection to operational response. Instead of stopping at visualization, we create an incident, assign verification work, and generate exportable evidence. The product is designed for Kazakhstan oil and gas teams that need a realistic screening and prioritization tool before field verification.`

## What Makes It Innovative

Short:

`Our innovation is not only anomaly detection. It is the closed workflow from signal to MRV evidence.`

Medium:

`Many competing solutions focus on dashboards, AI assistants, or map-based visualization. Saryna differentiates itself by completing the workflow: anomaly, incident, task, report. This makes the product more operationally relevant and more credible for ESG and regulatory use cases.`

## Why Now

Short:

`Methane, flaring, and MRV readiness are becoming more important at the same time for Kazakhstan oil and gas.`

Medium:

`Kazakhstan's oil and gas sector is under growing pressure to improve methane visibility, flaring discipline, and reporting readiness. This makes methane and flaring workflow tools timely not only for sustainability narratives, but also for operational prioritization and evidence generation.`

## Why Kazakhstan

Short:

`Kazakhstan needs practical MRV tools that speak the language of oil and gas operations, not generic climate dashboards.`

Medium:

`Saryna is designed around Kazakhstan's oil and gas context, where methane, flaring, ESG, and compliance concerns increasingly overlap. The product is intentionally positioned as an operational workflow layer that can fit a pilot environment without requiring a full enterprise rollout from day one.`

## Target Users

Short:

`Operations, integrity, ESG, and compliance teams inside oil and gas companies.`

Medium:

`The primary users are operations and integrity teams that need faster prioritization of abnormal signals. Secondary users are ESG and compliance teams that need a cleaner evidence trail and better reporting readiness. The product is designed to connect these groups through one workflow.`

## Product Scope

Use this wording:

`Saryna is a screening and operational prioritization layer. We do not claim exact equipment-level leak pinpointing from satellite data, and we do not position the product as a replacement for LDAR.`

## Value Proposition

Short:

`Saryna helps teams react faster, prioritize verification better, and produce cleaner MRV evidence.`

Medium:

`The value of Saryna comes from faster time to triage, clearer verification ownership, better ESG and compliance reporting readiness, and lower friction between operations and reporting teams. The goal is not only visibility, but accountable action.`

## Business Value Language

Use these phrases:

- faster time to triage
- reduced reporting friction
- lower regulatory exposure
- better verification prioritization
- stronger ESG reporting readiness

If asked for economics, say:

`The business case can be modeled as avoided response waste plus lower reporting friction plus reduced exposure from delayed action.`

## Current MVP Status

Short:

`The MVP already demonstrates the full workflow from anomaly to MRV report.`

Medium:

`The current MVP already includes a signal queue, incident creation, verification tasks, an audit trail, report generation, and a print-ready export path. It also includes a live CH4 screening proof path through Google Earth Engine, while keeping a seeded fallback for demo stability.`

## Current Data Sources

Use this wording:

- `Sentinel-5P / TROPOMI for CH4 screening`
- `VIIRS Nightfire for flare activity context`
- `CH4 translated into CO2e framing`

## Demo Differentiator

Short:

`The differentiator is the full loop: anomaly -> incident -> task -> MRV report.`

Medium:

`Most competing demos are likely to show a chatbot, a generic AI dashboard, or a map. Saryna stands out because it shows a complete workflow that looks closer to a real operational tool.`

## Team Execution Story

Short:

`We built the MVP around a realistic submission-first scope and focused on workflow maturity over feature sprawl.`

Medium:

`Our approach has been disciplined: protect the core demo loop, avoid enterprise distractions, and build the features that make the product look credible to oil and gas decision-makers. This is why the MVP already feels like a workflow product, not a prototype dashboard.`

## Roadmap After Submission

Use this wording:

`After submission, the next step is not a total rebuild. We keep the same workflow and deepen ingest reliability, asset context, and pilot integrations while preserving the anomaly-to-report loop.`

## What Not To Claim In The Form

Do not write:

- exact leak source detection from satellite data
- full replacement of LDAR
- complete enterprise deployment
- guaranteed cost savings without customer data
- magical AI automation

## Safe Closing Paragraph

`Saryna is built to help Kazakhstan oil and gas teams move from methane visibility to accountable action and MRV-ready evidence. By combining realistic satellite screening with a clear incident and verification workflow, the product offers a credible path from MVP to pilot.`
