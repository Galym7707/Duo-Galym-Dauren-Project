# Saryna MRV Pitch and Q&A Pack

Where we are: the MVP and demo loop are already assembled for submission week.  
Nearest goal: sound enterprise-relevant and Kazakhstan-specific on April 3, 2026 and April 9, 2026.  
Main risk: answering like a student dashboard team instead of an operations-and-MRV product team.

## Core Message

Use this sentence first:

`Saryna is an MRV workflow layer for methane and flaring visibility that turns satellite screening into incidents, verification tasks, and exportable evidence for Kazakhstan oil and gas teams.`

## 30-Second Opening

`Methane and flaring visibility is becoming an operational, ESG, and regulatory issue for Kazakhstan oil and gas. Today, teams often have maps, spreadsheets, and separate reporting workflows, but not one clear path from signal to action. Saryna solves that gap. We use satellite-based screening to identify priority anomalies, convert them into owned incidents, assign verification work, and generate MRV-ready evidence. We are not replacing LDAR. We are the screening and operational prioritization layer that helps companies react faster and report more defensibly.`

## 60-Second Expansion

`Our product starts where most competitors stop. Instead of showing only a map or an AI dashboard, we complete the workflow: anomaly, incident, task, MRV report. For Kazakhstan this matters now because methane reduction, flaring discipline, and MRV readiness are becoming more important in both ESG and regulatory conversations. We built the MVP to be honest about the data. Sentinel-5P is not a pinpoint instrument for one exact valve, so we position Saryna as a screening layer and pre-LDAR intelligence layer. That actually makes pilot adoption easier because the scope is realistic, the risk is lower, and the operational value is clear.`

- Kazakhstan is part of the Global Methane Pledge discussion context, so methane visibility is a live strategic topic.
- Kazakhstan's NDC 3.0 was registered in the UNFCCC context on November 26, 2025, which strengthens the climate and reporting narrative.
- Kazakhstan has also been positioned around Zero Routine Flaring by 2030, so flaring visibility and evidence workflows are timely.
- The product fits the practical language of PetroDigital and oil-and-gas management: risk, prioritization, verification, reporting, and auditability.

## What Makes Us Different

Say this clearly:

- Most teams will show a chatbot, a generic AI assistant, or a dashboard.
- We show an operational MRV workflow.
- Our differentiator is not the map. It is the closed loop: `anomaly -> incident -> task -> MRV report`.

## Likely Jury Questions

### 1. How is this different from a dashboard?

Short answer:

`A dashboard shows data; we turn a signal into an owned incident, verification work, and MRV-ready evidence.`

Longer answer:

`Our key difference is workflow closure. The product does not stop at anomaly visualization. It assigns operational ownership, structures verification, and produces a reportable evidence trail.`

### 2. Can you really identify the exact methane source?

Short answer:

`No, and we do not claim that. We are a screening layer that helps teams know where to investigate first.`

Longer answer:

`Sentinel-5P is valuable for anomaly screening and prioritization, not for exact equipment-level attribution. That is why our product is positioned as pre-LDAR intelligence and workflow support, which is more realistic for pilot adoption.`

### 3. Why is this relevant specifically for Kazakhstan?

Short answer:

`Because methane, flaring, MRV readiness, and ESG pressure are all becoming more important at the same time in Kazakhstan.`

Longer answer:

`Kazakhstan's climate and reporting context, including the NDC 3.0 timeframe after November 26, 2025, makes methane visibility and evidence workflows strategically relevant. The oil-and-gas sector needs tools that speak the language of operations and compliance, not only analytics.`

### 4. Who would actually use this inside a company?

Short answer:

`Operations, integrity, ESG, and compliance teams can all use the same workflow from different angles.`

Longer answer:

`Operations teams use it for prioritization, integrity teams for verification routing, and ESG or compliance teams for evidence and reporting readiness. The product is designed as a bridge between those groups.`

### 5. What happens after the MVP?

Short answer:

`After the MVP, we deepen ingest, asset context, and pilot integrations without changing the core workflow.`

Longer answer:

`The next phase is not a total rebuild. We keep the same anomaly-to-report loop and add more reliable source adapters, asset-level context, scheduling, and pilot-specific data integration.`

## Tough Questions

### Regulator

Question:
- `How do we know this is defensible and not just another ESG visualization?`

Answer:

`Because we are building an audit-friendly MRV workflow, not just a front-end view. Every step from screening to report is structured, attributable, and exportable.`

### Investor

Question:
- `Why would a customer pay for this if satellite data is open?`

Answer:

`Open data is not the product. The product is the operational workflow that reduces time-to-triage, structures verification, and lowers reporting friction.`

### CTO / IT Director

Question:
- `Can this integrate into a real enterprise environment later?`

Answer:

`Yes. The MVP is intentionally lightweight, but the architecture already separates frontend, API, pipeline logic, and evidence outputs so it can evolve into a pilot-ready stack.`

### Operations Leader

Question:
- `Will this create noise for field teams?`

Answer:

`Only if it is positioned badly. Our design goal is the opposite: a short queue, explicit prioritization, and clear ownership before dispatch.`
