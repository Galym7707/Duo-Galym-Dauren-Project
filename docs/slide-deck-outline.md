# Saryna MRV Slide Deck Outline

Where we are: by March 27, 2026, the MVP, demo flow, and Q&A materials are already in the repo.  
Nearest goal: structure the slide deck so the jury understands the product in under three minutes.  
Main risk: overloading the deck with analytics or architecture instead of reinforcing the workflow story.

## Deck Rule

If a slide does not strengthen the loop below, cut it:

`signal -> incident -> task -> MRV report`

## Recommended 7-Slide Structure

## Slide 1. Problem

Title:

`Methane visibility is not enough without an action workflow`

Main point:

`Kazakhstan oil and gas teams need not only anomaly detection, but a clear path from signal to verification and reporting.`

What to show:

- one short problem statement
- 2-3 pain points:
  - fragmented tools
  - slow response to abnormal signals
  - weak auditability for ESG / compliance

What not to show:

- long climate theory
- too many market statistics

Speaker note:

`The problem is not lack of data. The problem is lack of operational closure.`

## Slide 2. Why Now / Why Kazakhstan

Title:

`Why now for Kazakhstan`

Main point:

`Methane, flaring, MRV readiness, and ESG pressure are converging into one practical opportunity.`

What to show:

- Kazakhstan context from your pitch pack
- November 26, 2025 NDC 3.0 reference
- Global Methane Pledge / flaring / MRV framing

What not to show:

- too many logos
- weak generic sustainability language

Speaker note:

`This is not abstract climate tech. It is timely infrastructure for a real oil-and-gas reporting and operations context.`

## Slide 3. Product

Title:

`Saryna is an MRV workflow layer`

Main point:

`We turn screening signals into incidents, verification tasks, and exportable evidence.`

What to show:

- one-line product statement
- the 4-step chain:
  - anomaly
  - incident
  - task
  - MRV report

What not to show:

- “AI copilot” positioning as the headline
- raw architecture diagram

Speaker note:

`This is not another dashboard. This is the workflow that sits on top of screening data.`

## Slide 4. How It Works

Title:

`From satellite screening to MRV evidence`

Main point:

`Saryna combines methane and flare screening with an operational workflow that teams can actually act on.`

What to show:

- Sentinel-5P / TROPOMI for CH4
- VIIRS Nightfire for flare detection
- CH4 to CO2e framing
- workflow box:
  `screening -> incident -> verification -> report`

What not to show:

- too much backend detail
- anything that sounds like exact equipment attribution

Speaker note:

`We are honest about scope: screening first, verification next, evidence output last.`

## Slide 5. Demo

Title:

`What the team can do in the MVP`

Main point:

`The MVP already demonstrates the full action loop, not only analytics.`

What to show:

- one annotated product screenshot or 4-frame storyboard
- optional captions:
  - short queue
  - owned incident
  - task discipline
  - report export

What not to show:

- too many screens
- tiny UI details the jury cannot read from the room

Speaker note:

`If the screen is busy, simplify. The jury should understand it from the back of the hall.`

## Slide 6. Why We Win

Title:

`Why this is stronger than a dashboard`

Main point:

`Competitors may show AI or visualization, but we show a complete MRV-ready workflow.`

What to show:

- short comparison table:
  - generic dashboard
  - chatbot
  - Saryna workflow

Use rows like:

- anomaly visibility
- operational ownership
- verification tasks
- audit-ready output

Speaker note:

`Our advantage is not prettier analytics. It is workflow maturity.`

## Slide 7. Pilot Value / Next Step

Title:

`Pilot value for operators and regulators`

Main point:

`The MVP is ready to serve as a realistic pilot workflow for screening, prioritization, and evidence generation.`

What to show:

- value bullets:
  - faster time to triage
  - clearer verification ownership
  - better ESG reporting readiness
  - lower regulatory exposure
- next step:
  - pilot on selected assets
  - deepen ingest
  - connect operator context

Speaker note:

`Do not promise a giant rollout. Promise a credible pilot with realistic scope.`

## Optional Slide 8. Team / Execution

Use only if needed.

Title:

`Why we can execute`

Main point:

`We already built the MVP around a realistic submission-first scope and can turn it into a pilot-ready workflow.`

What to show:

- team strengths
- MVP already built
- clear next steps after the contest

What not to show:

- inflated roadmap
- enterprise fantasies

## Slide Order Checklist

Before finalizing, check:

1. Does the deck explain the problem in oil-and-gas language?
2. Does it sound Kazakhstan-specific?
3. Does Slide 3 clearly say this is an MRV workflow layer?
4. Does the deck avoid overclaiming satellite precision?
5. Does the demo slide reinforce the exact product loop?
6. Does the final slide promise a pilot, not a fantasy rollout?

## Lines To Reuse

Opening:

`Saryna is an MRV workflow layer for methane and flaring visibility that turns screening signals into incidents, verification tasks, and exportable evidence.`

Scope honesty:

`We do not replace LDAR. We help teams know where to investigate first and how to document the response.`

Differentiation:

`Most teams stop at visibility. We continue into ownership, verification, and reporting.`

Closing:

`Saryna helps Kazakhstan oil and gas teams move from methane visibility to accountable action and MRV-ready evidence.`
